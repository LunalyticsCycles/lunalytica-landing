The Problem

Generic supplement advice isn't working — because your hormones aren't generic.

Most women with hormonal concerns — whether they're trying to conceive, managing PCOS, navigating perimenopause, or dealing with cycle irregularities — end up piecing together supplement protocols from Google, Reddit, or a 10-minute doctor appointment. The advice is well-meaning but one-size-fits-all. And hormones don't work that way.


What your body needs in your luteal phase is different from what someone else needs. What helps PCOS looks different from what helps perimenopause. The difference between a protocol that actually works and one that doesn't often comes down to whether it was built for you — or for nobody in particular.
